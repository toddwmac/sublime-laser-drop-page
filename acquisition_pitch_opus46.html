Executive Summary

A Rare Opportunity in Medical Device Contract Manufacturing

Sublime Laser, Inc. is a Salt Lake City-based precision laser machining company focused exclusively on the medical device sector. Founded by CEO Todd Turnlund, the company has built decades of expertise cutting micro-scale tubular components — stents, guidewire reinforcements, and other vascular implants — from nitinol, stainless steel, precious metals, ceramics, and glass.

The company operates both femtosecond lasers (cold ablation, near-zero HAZ) and ultrafast fiber lasers (high-throughput production), giving it a dual-capability platform rare among small-scale contract manufacturers. Its ISO 13485 certification, rapid turnaround model, and "Rent a Laser + Engineer" service create multiple revenue channels and deep customer stickiness with R&D teams at major OEMs.

Both femtosecond (precision/R&D) and fiber (speed/production) platforms allow seamless scaling from prototype to volume. Customers don't need to re-qualify a different vendor when transitioning phases.

  • Large competitors (Resonetics, etc.) can pursue aggressive pricing

Emerging Trends

Macro Trends Amplifying Sublime's Value

Multiple converging trends make the next 3–5 years an inflection point for precision laser machining in medical devices.

🧊

Cold Ablation Becomes the Standard

As device geometries shrink below 1mm OD, thermal laser cutting introduces unacceptable HAZ. Femtosecond (cold ablation) is rapidly transitioning from "nice to have" to "must have" — putting Sublime's core technology at the center of demand.

🤖

AI-Driven Process Optimization

Machine learning is being applied to laser parameter tuning — optimizing power, pulse rate, scan speed, and gas flow in real-time. Sublime's data from thousands of cuts across materials positions it to leverage this trend early.

🌐

Supply Chain Reshoring

Post-pandemic, OEMs are actively reshoring precision manufacturing from Asia to domestic, FDA-regulated suppliers. ISO 13485-certified U.S. shops like Sublime are direct beneficiaries of this structural shift.

🔬

Drug-Eluting & Bioresorbable Devices

Next-generation stents and scaffolds require laser machining of polymer and composite tubes — materials where femtosecond lasers excel due to thermal sensitivity. This opens entirely new product families for Sublime.

Applied AI Labs · Prospecting intelligence

From database search to defensible outreach.

The system’s response identifies the right problem: an FDA record can reveal a company or device program, but it does not prove purchasing authority, budget, timing, supplier fit, or contact accuracy. This brief builder turns those concerns into explicit evidence gates.

Treat every regulatory or market signal as a lead—not as proof of buying intent.
01 · Foundations

Define the mission

Give the system enough context to distinguish a useful prospect from a broad list of medical device companies.

Evidence gate
Why this matters. “Find hypotube buyers” is too broad. The search needs a target market, geography, account profile, and a clear definition of success.
02 · Fit definition

Describe the offering

The better the manufacturing and commercial fit is defined, the more accurately accounts can be ranked.

Required context
Why this matters. A company may develop catheters but still be a poor fit because its geometry, material, volume, quality, or supplier qualification requirements do not match the offering.
03 · Signal selection

Choose the data sources

Document which sources should be used, what each source can reveal, and what it cannot prove.

Evidence gate
System’s question: There are many FDA databases. Which ones should be targeted, and why are they the right sources for this search? Select sources based on the signal they provide—not because they are merely available.
04 · Buying center

Define purchasing authority

Specify which people matter, what authority means, and how a title will be validated.

Evidence gate
System’s question: How do you confirm that an individual has purchasing authority? A title alone is not enough. The system should distinguish economic buyers, technical evaluators, supplier-quality stakeholders, and useful internal champions.
05 · Commercial readiness

Confirm project activity and budget

Capture the evidence that a target may have an active program and a realistic path to supplier engagement.

Evidence gate
System’s question: How do you confirm that the company is working on a project with budget? Public data rarely proves budget directly. Use observable proxies, record their source and date, and distinguish “unknown” from “confirmed.”
06 · Opportunity timing

Match acquisition stage to the offering

Define when a company is early enough to influence and mature enough to have an actionable need.

Evidence gate
System’s question: How do you confirm that the opportunity is in a stage that matches the offering? A company can be relevant but too early, already locked into a supplier, or too late for a new component source.
07 · Data quality

Set the contact validation standard

Define the minimum contact record and how recently each data point must have been verified.

Evidence gate
System’s question: How do you confirm that contact information is accurate? Every contact should have a role source, a company association, a verification date, and a confidence level. Do not treat an inferred email address as verified.
08 · Research output

Candidate account and contact log

Use this worksheet to record the company, device evidence, project signal, and person of interest without losing the provenance of the research.

Working list
Recommended rule: Every candidate should have at least one source URL, a dated signal, and a clear note explaining why the company may need a laser-cut hypotube.

09 · Operating model

Design the AI-assisted workflow

Separate what AI can accelerate from what a human must verify before a prospect is contacted.

Target → enrich
Creative opportunity. AI can classify device records, extract applicant names, normalize device terminology, find repeated company signals, deduplicate accounts, rank evidence, and draft personalization. It should not invent authority, budget, product requirements, or contact verification.
10 · Outreach preparation

Give the system a relevant opening

These fields provide the evidence and hypotheses needed to draft a specific introductory email without making unsupported claims.

Email inputs
Important. The outreach should reference a verified device, program, or business signal—not simply say that the recipient works in medical devices.
